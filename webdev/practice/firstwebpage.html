Eagle Project: Getting it done without tears!

Setting your self up to succeed

The best thing you can do is to make it digital, and make a worksheet that works for YOUR brain. Now, this is when you say "but the packet is just a giant worksheet. Why cant I use that?" Well that isn't going to work. For starters trying to edit that on the fly and in the field is a nightmare. It also isn't built for you to be able to easily understand. It's meant to be official and easy for readers to understand. This is YOUR eagle project so make a version for YOU to understand and then fill out the official one later. So there are a few steps you'll want to do to get organized and never lose things:

Step 1: make a google drive folder (or the equivalent on another software program similar) and keep EVERYTHING in it

Step 2: Make a spread sheet to keep track of time. When doing your project feel free to print it out, but remember to IMIDIATLY fill out any new entries on the online version. A digital record cant get lost.

Step 3: Keep track of the money. I cannot tell you how many scouts lose a reciet or have to guess how much something costs. Take a picture of your reciets IMEDIATLY AFTER GETTING THEM so they dont get lost then log them in a spreadsheet. When the time comes to fill out the packet you will have all the information you need and wont have to guess or worry about lost reciets or who paid what and what happened that one time. It will take you maybe an hour of copy/paste what it took the others before you days.

Step 4 (optional, use if you are going to stop and start a lot or are forgetfull): Make a document to keep track of your project. This is where you can write down your project ideas, your research, and your progress. You can also use it to keep track of any changes you make to your project (yes it's in the final packet). This is also where you can write down any questions you have for your mentor or for the people you are working with or any notes from meetings or conversations you have about your project.

Motivational Issues

"Just start now!" "Get it done!" "Schedule a time!" Sound familiar? We know they are right but seriously PAPERWORK IS BORING! Here's how to get the ball rolling and keep it rolling:

1: get a buddy. Things are always better with friends right? When working on your eagle project find another scout to do it with you. Be eachothers accountability partner and if possible sit down together and do your paperwork together. Just having them there as they work on their own will make it easier.

2: Seriously, set a schedule. You know there is at least one hour a week you could spare. USE IT. A set aside time means the project isn't getting set aside. Don't wait till the deadline, wait till your set aside time. You'll have to do meetings and the actual project outside of that time but other than that it is incredable how much wright it takes off your shoulders.

3: Celabrate the milestones. At NLYT it is taught to celabrate the milestone to keep the teams modivation high. Now I swar by that sylubus only because it works. Bites out of an elephant are still bites
